Cloud roundup: our best cloud posts of 2014

Posted December 31, 2014December 23, 2014
  • 0
  • 0
  • 0

The cloud is one of the most disruptive forces in modern business. It’s also one of the biggest buzzwords, and there may be as much confusion about the cloud as there are groundbreaking developments.

Here is a roundup of our most popular cloud posts of 2014. These will help you untangle fact from marketing speak, and understand what the cloud is, why it’s important, and when you might consider the cloud for your business.

Un-clouding the hybrid cloud: Connecting public and private clouds

Microsoft cloud expert Neha Bajwa explains the difference between public, private, and hybrid clouds in clear and certain terms. If you’re confused about the difference, you’ll want to read this to help uncloud the hybrid cloud.

Putting the cloud under lock and key

One of the biggest concerns decision makers cite about moving to the cloud is security. Microsoft executive VP & general counsel Brad Smith walks you through cloud privacy and data security, and how to keep your cloud solution compliant.

On the verge of a breakthrough: how the cloud helps your business find insights in big data

One of the most valuable reasons for adopting cloud technology is to start gleaning insights from big data. This post walks you through several examples of how different organizations are using cloud technology to sift through and get value from big data.

How the cloud protects your data from natural disasters

It may start with earthquakes (and birds and snakes), but natural disasters don’t have to end your company’s data. With a cloud backup solution, a disaster is not the end of the world as you know it—so you can feel fine about keeping your data safe.

Cloud: the key to mobility

With an increasingly mobile workforce, cloud solutions help enable your workers to engage no matter where they are. Empowering your employees to work remotely increases productivity, and this infographic has all the information you need to know about enabling mobility through the cloud.
