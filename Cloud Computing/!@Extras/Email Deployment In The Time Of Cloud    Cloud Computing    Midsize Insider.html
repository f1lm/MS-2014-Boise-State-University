Email Deployment In The Time Of Cloud

By Athar Afzal | Nov 12, 2014
Topic: Cloud Computing

When your business began, it is very likely physical servers were the only existing option for application deployment. Today, with the addition of virtualized servers and various implementations of cloud, businesses have the ability to access their necessary enterprise applications as they see fit.

Email is a familiar tool to almost everyone working today. In fact, the majority of businesses view email as the most important application in their business, as it is an inescapable reality of the modern world. How do businesses moving towards composability utilize the cloud for their email application deployment?

Make no mistake: at least a third of businesses still deploy email in the traditional manner, on a physical server. However, the vast majority of businesses deploy their email applications in the cloud. There are many benefits to the public cloud, such as ease of access from multiple devices and locations, as well as ease of deployment, and scalability for increased storage and users. However, as with all cloud deployments, users and IT managers must be aware of data security issues and remain vigilant. Software as a Service (SaaS) is a very popular choice. The fact that so many organizations utilize SaaS is not surprising; it offers a quick and easy deployment. Users can quickly add email accounts for new employees and shut them off as they leave, paying only for active email accounts.  

A small number of organizations choose to deploy their email application in the hosted cloud. This is an attractive option for a large organization that can use an entire server to host their email account. The deployment would be dedicated to the single organization with no comingling of data with other organizations. The organization controls the data and the software deployment and an email service company provides the servers and local tech support. Mobile users can still access their email accounts with the same security controls as those behind the firewall.

Hybrid cloud is the newest form of cloud service, and is the combination of a business’s internal cloud and a public cloud deployment. Ideally, this combination would be seamless; however in reality, the hybrid cloud requires a certain degree of specialized IT skills, of which many businesses may not have access. When executed properly, it can include many of the benefits of both types of cloud, the privacy of an internal private cloud, and the scalability of the Public cloud. Few businesses have adopted the hybrid cloud because they do not yet feel they have the necessary skills to perform a successful hybrid deployment for such a critical business application. However, if a business (particularly one moving towards a composable model) has the skills and resources for a hybrid cloud deployment, it is an option worth considering. Although there are pitfalls, it can combine the best of the public cloud with the security of a private cloud.
