Is Application Security Testing in the Cloud a Good Fit for Your Organization?

Why Do Organizations Consider Application Security Testing in the Cloud?

The IT world continues to evolve, and like many of your colleagues, you’ve decided to jump on the cloud train. Perhaps you did so for the following reasons:

  • You want to reduce IT costs.
  • You manage a global team, and the infrastructure of the cloud lets you collaborate more effectively in a distributed environment.
  • You’re frightened by the prospect of managing security on your own, and you’d prefer to engage industry experts to help secure your business applications.

These are all great reasons, and, of course, there are many more for you to consider. This article will concentrate on another — arguably the most important — reason for your organization to make the move to the cloud: enhanced security protection.

Making the Case for Application Security Testing in the Cloud

When developing and deploying applications in the cloud, you can leverage security services to significantly extend your organization’s data protection at an affordable cost. Application vulnerability scanning is one specific area that you certainly don’t want to miss out on. In the past, companies couldn’t conceive of running application vulnerability scanning in cloud-based infrastructures out of fear that it was too risky to expose highly confidential information; that scanning activities were too complicated; and that automated service results might be prone to errors and return data useless for analysis.

Essentially, organizations believed they had only these three choices:

  1. To form security teams and manage their own security with on-premises tools;
  2. To seek outside help in the form of external penetration testing teams or managed services providers;
  3. Do nothing and hope their limited network security protection is sufficient.

These options all obviously have their pros and cons, but we would like to propose a fourth choice that you should consider: automated services for application vulnerability scanning.

Automated Application Vulnerability Detection

Does it make sense for your organization to utilize automated cloud services? As with many technical scenarios, the answer to that question is, “It depends.” Why is that? If you aren’t currently performing application security scanning activities and are instead relying on the “hoping” strategy outlined above, then yes, automated services make a lot of sense. With recent technical improvements made in this space, automated services produce very reliable and easy-to-interpret results while generating thorough reports with low false-positive rates.

However, if you can afford the higher cost of addressing application vulnerabilities with on-premise solutions or managed services offerings, it is recommended that you rely on automated scanning for one-off scans or special projects. In any event, you should still engage in cost and functionality comparisons of on-premise solutions and managed services against cloud-based automated services, because automated services generally offer additional convenience and flexibility.

The primary value of self-service, automated scanning is not in how safe and accurate the services are. Rather, it’s in the way they help you improve your overall security posture, with limited effort required on your team’s end. Being lightweight and easy to use, these solutions can be easily integrated into your development life cycle. And, as you know, the earlier you detect security vulnerabilities in the development process, the easier and less expensive it is to remediate them.

Because of the generally higher costs of managed services, organizations don’t often require them early in the game. However, they can get a good sense of their overall application security preparedness through the use of automated services. Over time, the most logical step for most organizations is to leverage cloud services wherever and whenever they make sense. By their very nature, automated security scanning services will typically prove to be safer than many other options, particularly the much-followed but seldom discussed “network hope” option.

Protecting Your Brand Image and Mission-Critical Data

If you value your business’s reputation, the protection of your consumers’ data and your untarnished brand image, you must include application vulnerability scanning in your security arsenal. If you don’t, sooner or later, a cybercriminal will target your organization and you’ll be left defenseless.
