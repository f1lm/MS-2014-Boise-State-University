Nuage Networks: Pets, Cattle and Herding Dogs

Randy Bias and Bill Baker first illustrated the parallel of cloud applications to "pets and cattle&rdquo;,&nbsp;by setting the primitives for scale-out and applications that can tolerate failure.&nbsp;For the cloud applications, (ie cattle), an often forgotten aspect though is the&nbsp;"herding dogs", or simply the control plane that will manage the lifecycle of&nbsp;the cloud and the applications. And as in every cattle, the herding dogs are critical&nbsp;for the survival of the cattle, so is the control plane in a scale out cloud. In this talk&nbsp;will focus specifically on the network control plane and outline the key requirements&nbsp;in terms of performance, reliability, and scale. We will then focus&nbsp;specifically on OpenStack Neutron and present some examples of functions&nbsp;that result in asymptotically quadratic operations that significantly limit&nbsp;scalability. We will present alternative implementations based on SDN controllers&nbsp;that remove these bottlenecks from the Neutron server, and&nbsp;enable the seamless scale of OpenStack clouds. &nbsp;
