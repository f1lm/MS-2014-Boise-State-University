Capacity Planning for Virtual Environments

By Kelly Meade | Oct 29, 2014
Topic: Cloud Computing

Managing virtual environments is a critical operation in IT organizations, and one of their continuing challenges is finding a way to appropriately optimize their infrastructure. High capital costs are pushing organizations to become better at managing virtual environments, because they are feeling the pressure to minimize capital spend on new hardware. Organizations that use capacity planning do a better job of managing and getting the most out of their virtual environments, compared to those that do not. Being able to virtualize more applications and achieve a higher average server utilization means that they are wasting less infrastructure and are more efficiently using their resources, even as their virtual environment grows.

The insight that capacity planning gives IT into their virtual environment also helps to manage Virtual Machine (VM) sprawl, through its detailed information about the number of VM's and their utilization. This assists administrators in determining which VM's are actually necessary for the organization, allowing them to allocate their infrastructure resources more efficiently. Organizations that have had virtualization for a long time are more likely to be utilizing a capacity planning tool. Capacity planning provides a direct ROI for organizations utilizing it because of the advantage it gives organizations in optimizing their virtual environments. Organizations using a capacity planning tool are more likely to notice a reduction in the cost of server and application deployment than organizations that do not. This shows that even mature organizations, which have already seen much of the up-front reduction in cost from virtualization, are still able to decrease costs by implementing more mature management tools.

The benefits of capacity planning all go back to helping organizations optimize their virtual environments and take advantage of all of the benefits that virtualization can provide. Virtualization management is difficult and having the right tools is critical to achieving and, more importantly, maintaining an optimal virtual environment.
