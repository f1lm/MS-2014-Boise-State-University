            Refactoring is so crucial for long-term projects that it easily overshadow any other feature you might ask for.

            We are all aware of the cycle:

            design->code->debug/test and back to design.

            but the correct way should be design->code->debug/test->refactor

            IMO about 30% of the time you had spent on coding should be planned for refactoring. and that is what I refer to as casual refactoring effort. (what you addressed)

            There is also the targeted refactoring effort, when you start identifying modules/places in your app where bugs accumulate more than the rest ..

            You said: "Small refactoring is good", I must add

            "Big refactoring is even better"

            To afford to refactor is to afford to change, which demands some security that any regressions will not be introduced, for this reason unit test and testing in general is a PRE!

            It is exactly BIG-refactoring what bring the BIG value to both dev team and clients.

            case study 1: since requirements are keep coming a new better design becomes applicable and even recommended. you need to redesign -> refactor

            case study 2: requirements remain the same, but a new insights to the problem or all together different approach might lead to cutting a code base 80%. DO IT! less code less maintenance, simpler testing....
