    Thanks for this Barbara. A question about syntax use however. You say:

    "In terms of the syntax you utilize to mark up your event listings, it can be done in either microdata or JSON-LD, whichever you prefer. Decide which syntax you want to use and stick with it. (In my personal opinion, JSON-LD is easier.)"

    While I'm a big fan of JSON-LD I've yet to see an example in the wild of a Google rich snippet generated from JSON-LD for any but the narrowly prescribed classes for which Google has currently explicitly given support (contact phone number and certain classes of music events). Given this, can you fully recommend JSON-LD for classes things such as review or products with offers where rich snippet generation is at least one of the goals of the webmaster. Or am I mistaken in thinking that JSON-LD isn't yet generating rich snippets - and if so, can you provide supporting evidence? Thanks!

        Barbara Starr Aaron Bradley • 6 months ago

        Hi Aaron,

        My understanding is that there is currently full support for type MusicEvent in terms of JSON-LD markup (and also schema.org actions I believe, but would need to double check that). All other types / domains in Google require microdata at this point in time/ I am hoping that JSON-LD support will migrate to other domains/departments in Google (perhaps by year end - but only time will tell).

        However, from an implementation POV (personal opinion) JSON-LD is so much easier, it is worth understanding and looking into, and then moving to other supported schema.org types when necessary.
