                                                                            John Yohalem 17 hours ago

                                                                            We know very little of western Europe and north Africa before the Phoenician, Greek and Roman colonial outposts were set up there. But there are megaliths all over the region -- as far west as the Canary Islands -- and the Sahara is far dryer now than it was in Roman times. Spain and France, the Low Countries and Scandinavia were dotted with megaliths, as well as the British Isles, and this culture extended to Sardinia and Malta in the Med and probably to the Maghrib. Just before the Carthaginians and the Romans invaded it to fight each other, Spain was a prosperous land mining many ores, with a huge and civilized culture sometimes called "Tartessos," of which the Dama de Elche is the most famous surviving artifact. But their written language (if they had one) was put on some perishable material not on stone or baked clay, and their great urban centers are now underneath Cadiz harbor. Sardinia and Malta are full of mysterious ancient artifacts. Will we ever have a real narrative of these cultures, a real understanding of their existence and its position before conquerors from the east destroyed them?
